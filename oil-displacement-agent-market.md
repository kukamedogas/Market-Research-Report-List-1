<p><ul><li>Anionic Type</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The oil displacement agent market primarily consists of two types: anionic and others. Anionic oil displacement agents utilize negatively charged surfactants to reduce interfacial tension, enhancing oil recovery from reservoirs. They are effective in low-salinity environments. Other types include non-ionic, cationic, and amphoteric agents, each tailored for specific conditions, such as varying salinity levels or temperature variations. These diverse formulations aim to improve oil extraction efficiency and are crucial in maximizing hydrocarbon recovery in various geological settings.</p></p>
