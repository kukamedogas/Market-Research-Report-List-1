<p><p>The wearable waist exoskeleton market is categorized into two primary types: passive support and active support exoskeletons. Passive support exoskeletons provide stability and weight distribution using a rigid structure without any powered assistance, making them suitable for lighter tasks and prolonged wear. In contrast, active support exoskeletons utilize motors and sensors to actively assist users by providing enhanced mobility and strength, ideal for demanding tasks in industrial or rehabilitation settings, ultimately improving user performance and reducing fatigue.</p></p>
